# What is CPM?

CPM is cost per thousand impressions: spend × 1000 / impressions. Meta
charges by impression in most auction modes, so CPM is roughly the price
the ad system is paying to put your ad in front of a thousand people.

CPM rises as competition for your audience rises (other advertisers
bidding harder), or as Meta deprioritizes your ad (lower quality
ranking). It also rises seasonally — Black Friday, Q4 holiday, US
election cycles.

The `cpm_saturation` heuristic fires when the recent 7-day CPM exceeds
the prior 7-day window by more than 20%. Pair this with a frequency or
quality-ranking signal before paying serious attention — a one-off CPM
jump on its own can be benign.
