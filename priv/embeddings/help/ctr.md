# What is CTR?

CTR (click-through rate) is the share of impressions that produce a click.
CTR = clicks / impressions, expressed as a percentage. A 0.02 CTR means
2% of people who saw your ad clicked it.

Meta does not have one universal CTR benchmark. Conversion-objective ads
typically run 0.5%–2%. Engagement objectives are higher; broad-reach video
is often lower. Treat CTR as a relative signal — compare an ad against its
own history (the honeymoon baseline) before comparing across ads.

A drop in CTR while frequency rises is the classic creative-fatigue signal.
The Creative Fatigue Predictor combines those two factors with quality
ranking and CPM to estimate fatigue confidence.
