# Ad frequency

Frequency is impressions divided by reach: how many times, on average, a
person saw your ad in the time window. A frequency of 3 means each person
in the reached audience saw the ad three times.

Frequency above 3.5 over a 7-day window is a creative-fatigue warning sign.
Above 5 is usually a clear pause-or-rotate signal — the audience has seen
enough of the same creative that incremental impressions buy diminishing
returns.

Lifetime frequency on a long-running ad climbs naturally; the metric that
matters is the recent 7-day average, paired with whether CTR is falling.
